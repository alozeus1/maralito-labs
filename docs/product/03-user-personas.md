# 03 · User Personas

Seven primary personas. Each includes goals, pain points, trust concerns, common purchases, payment concerns, delivery expectations, and support needs. (Names illustrative; the Stitch home greets "Hola Maria.")

> Cross-cutting truths: all personas are in/near **Ciudad Juárez**, value **EN/ES bilingual** service, and are won or lost on **trust + transparency**. Spanish is a first-class language, not a translation afterthought.

---

## Persona 1 — María, the Juárez Consumer (core)
*38, Juárez, shops U.S. brands for herself and home; smartphone-first.*
- **Goals:** Buy U.S. products she can't get (or that cost more) locally, without crossing; know the total cost up front; receive reliably at home.
- **Pain points:** Crossing is time-consuming; informal options have hidden fees and no proof; never sure what duties she'll owe.
- **Trust concerns:** "Will the right item arrive, undamaged and genuine? Is my money safe? Who do I talk to if something's wrong?"
- **Common purchases:** Apparel, beauty, small electronics, home goods, kids' items.
- **Payment concerns:** Wants secure card payment in a currency she understands; fears overcharging or surprise fees after the fact.
- **Delivery expectations:** Door delivery in Juárez within a few days of reception; clear ETA; notifications in Spanish.
- **Support needs:** Fast, human, Spanish-language concierge via WhatsApp; reassurance at each step.

## Persona 2 — Diego, the Student
*21, university student in Juárez, budget-conscious but brand-aware.*
- **Goals:** Get U.S.-priced tech, sneakers, textbooks; split or minimize fees; track cheaply.
- **Pain points:** Limited budget; no visa or no time to cross; sensitive to fees eating savings.
- **Trust concerns:** Worried about scams; wants to see reviews/proof; cares the item is authentic.
- **Common purchases:** Electronics/accessories, sneakers, gaming, textbooks, small gadgets.
- **Payment concerns:** May use a debit card or shared family card; wants the lowest justifiable fee and clarity on whether duties make it worth it.
- **Delivery expectations:** Flexible on speed if it's cheaper; fine with hub pickup option; status updates via app/WhatsApp.
- **Support needs:** Self-service answers, clear fee breakdown, chat support; low-friction reorders.

## Persona 3 — Lucía, the Parent / Family Shopper
*34, mother of two, manages household buying.*
- **Goals:** Source kids' clothing, school supplies, baby products, seasonal gifts from U.S. stores; reliable timing around school/holidays.
- **Pain points:** No time to cross with kids; needs predictability; juggling multiple items/orders.
- **Trust concerns:** Safety of children's products (right size/model), that gifts arrive on time and intact.
- **Common purchases:** Children's apparel/shoes, toys, baby gear, school supplies, holiday gifts.
- **Payment concerns:** Family budget; wants to avoid surprise duties; values money-back protection.
- **Delivery expectations:** Predictable delivery windows; reliable for time-sensitive (birthdays, back-to-school); home delivery preferred.
- **Support needs:** Reassuring, proactive updates; easy way to adjust/cancel; Spanish concierge.

## Persona 4 — Carlos, the Small Business Buyer
*45, owns a Juárez retail/repair shop; buys U.S. stock and parts.*
- **Goals:** Procure inventory/parts/equipment from U.S. suppliers reliably; predictable landed cost; documentation for his books.
- **Pain points:** Inconsistent informal channels; needs receipts/invoices and **RFC/tax-ID** handling; cash-flow sensitivity to duties.
- **Trust concerns:** Correct items/quantities, commercial-invoice accuracy, customs compliance for business goods.
- **Common purchases:** Electronics components, tools, retail stock, replacement parts, equipment.
- **Payment concerns:** Wants invoices with **RFC**, clear duties, possibly net terms later; larger order values.
- **Delivery expectations:** Reliable, scheduled delivery to his business; freight/pallet for bigger loads (Business Delivery service).
- **Support needs:** A dedicated/priority contact; documentation support; bulk/recurring order handling.

## Persona 5 — Andrea, the Frequent Cross-Border Shopper
*30, already shops U.S. constantly via informal means; high intent, high frequency.*
- **Goals:** Replace her patchwork of friends/forwarders with one reliable service; speed + convenience; track everything in one place.
- **Pain points:** Tired of coordinating crossings and chasing packages; wants consolidation and history.
- **Trust concerns:** Consistency over time; will it be reliable on the 10th order, not just the 1st?
- **Common purchases:** Wide range — fashion, beauty, electronics, home; high volume.
- **Payment concerns:** Wants saved payment methods, fast checkout, fair fees at volume; possible loyalty/VIP value.
- **Delivery expectations:** Fast, consolidated, predictable; values reorder + order history; premium/VIP perks.
- **Support needs:** Low-touch when things work, instant escalation when they don't; recognizes and rewards her loyalty.

## Persona 6 — Rosa, the Customer Without a U.S. Visa
*50, cannot cross the border; this is her only practical access to U.S. goods.*
- **Goals:** Access U.S. products she otherwise simply can't get; total dependence on a trustworthy intermediary.
- **Pain points:** No alternative if BorderPass fails her; may be less tech-confident; anxious about the unknown process.
- **Trust concerns:** Highest of all personas — she's handing money and trust to a company she can't physically verify; needs maximum reassurance, proof, and human contact.
- **Common purchases:** Everyday and special items unavailable or pricier locally; gifts for family.
- **Payment concerns:** Security of payment; fear of being scammed; wants guarantees and clear recourse.
- **Delivery expectations:** Clear, simple, Spanish-language updates; home delivery; a human she can call.
- **Support needs:** Patient, proactive, human-led support; simple UX; strong trust signals (inspection photos, concierge, money-back).

## Persona 7 — Ana, the Business Procurement User
*41, operations/purchasing for a mid-size Juárez company.*
- **Goals:** Systematic, documented procurement of U.S. goods; multiple orders; approvals and reporting; landed-cost predictability.
- **Pain points:** Needs process, documentation, and accountability — not an ad-hoc consumer flow; manages spend and compliance.
- **Trust concerns:** Compliance and customs correctness at scale; accurate commercial invoices; audit trail; reliability for operations.
- **Common purchases:** Equipment, components, bulk supplies, freight/pallet shipments.
- **Payment concerns:** Invoicing with **RFC**, possibly purchase orders/net terms (future), higher values, finance approvals.
- **Delivery expectations:** Scheduled business delivery, freight handling, documentation for each shipment.
- **Support needs:** Account-level relationship, priority support, reporting, multi-user/role access (future B2B features).

## Persona → product implications (summary)

| Persona | Highest-leverage trust signal | Key MVP need | Notable future need |
|---------|-------------------------------|--------------|---------------------|
| María (consumer) | Inspection photos + transparent quote | Core buy-for-me + tracking | Loyalty |
| Diego (student) | Clear fee/duty math | Low-fee clarity, hub pickup | Cheap consolidation |
| Lucía (parent) | Predictable timing + money-back | Reliable delivery windows | Reorder for seasons |
| Carlos (SMB) | Invoice/RFC + customs accuracy | Receipts + RFC handling | Net terms, recurring |
| Andrea (frequent) | Consistency + history | Saved payment + reorder | VIP/loyalty |
| Rosa (no visa) | Human concierge + proof | Maximum reassurance UX | Voice/phone support |
| Ana (procurement) | Documentation + audit trail | Business delivery + invoices | Multi-user B2B, POs |
